Website Pre-Writing · Planning Template

"How Social Media Impacts Teen Mental Health"

01

Essential Questions

Q1 Question

How does social media impact teenagers' mental health?

Q2 Question

Does increased social media use lead to higher levels of anxiety and depression in teens?

Q3 Question

What are the positive and negative effects of social media on teen well-being?

02

Introduction / Homepage

00

Homepage

Purpose
Introduce the topic and explain why it matters to teenagers today.
Main Ideas
  • Social media is used by most teenagers every day.
  • Apps like TikTok, Instagram, Snapchat, and YouTube are a major part of communication and entertainment.
  • Social media can positively and negatively affect mental health.
  • Teens often do not realize how much social media changes their emotions, confidence, sleep, and stress levels.
  • The website will teach users how social media affects teen mental health and how to build healthier habits online.
Visual Ideas
  • Hero image of a teenager using a phone
  • Large title banner
  • Navigation buttons
  • Clean modern design with blue / purple color palette
03

Site Sections

01

Why This Topic Matters

Main Ideas
  • Teen brains are still developing emotionally and socially.
  • Social media creates constant access to opinions, comparisons, and feedback.
  • Many teens spend several hours a day online.
  • Mental health concerns among teens have increased in recent years.
  • Learning healthy online habits can improve confidence and emotional well-being.
Key Fact
Teens who spend more than 3 hours daily on social media may face increased risks of anxiety and depression.
Visual Ideas
  • Statistic cards
  • Infographic showing average screen time
  • Mental health themed graphics
02

Positive Effects of Social Media

Main Ideas
  • Helps teens stay connected with friends and family.
  • Allows creativity through videos, art, music, and posts.
  • Gives access to support groups and communities.
  • Helps teens express themselves and discover interests.
  • Spreads awareness about important events and causes.
  • Can help shy people communicate more comfortably.
Examples
Group chats Educational videos Creative communities Online support spaces
Visual Ideas
  • Positive-themed cards / icons
  • Images of teens collaborating or communicating
03

Negative Effects of Social Media

Main Ideas
  • Social comparison can lower self-esteem.
  • Filters and edited photos create unrealistic expectations.
  • Cyberbullying can seriously affect emotional health.
  • Addiction to scrolling wastes time and increases stress.
  • Notifications and online pressure can create anxiety.
  • Using phones late at night affects sleep.
  • Harmful content can negatively influence teens.
Examples
Comparing appearance / popularity Pressure over likes & followers Doomscrolling Online harassment
Visual Ideas
  • Darker contrasting section colors
  • Stress / anxiety graphics
  • Phone notification visuals
04

Research and Statistics

Purpose
Show real evidence and research instead of only opinions.
Sources Used
  • U.S. Surgeon General
  • Pew Research Center
  • Book source: iGen by Jean Twenge
Important Statistics
  • Most teens use social media daily.
  • Many teens say they are online "almost constantly."
  • YouTube, TikTok, Instagram, and Snapchat are the most popular apps.
  • Increased screen time is linked to higher mental health risks.
Visual Ideas
  • Charts
  • Animated counters
  • Graphs and percentage visuals
05

Interactive Self-Check Activity

Purpose
Help the audience reflect on their own habits.
Questions
  1. Do I check my phone immediately after waking up?
  2. Do I lose track of time while scrolling?
  3. Do I compare myself to others online?
  4. Do social media interactions affect my mood?
  5. Do I use social media late at night?
Reflection
Users will receive a message encouraging healthier habits and awareness.
Visual Ideas
  • Interactive checkboxes
  • Reflection popup / message box
06

Healthy Social Media Habits

Goal
Teach students practical ways to improve their relationship with social media.
Main Ideas
  • Set screen time limits.
  • Avoid scrolling before bed.
  • Turn off unnecessary notifications.
  • Unfollow accounts that create stress.
  • Follow educational or positive creators.
  • Take breaks from apps when needed.
  • Spend time offline with friends and family.
  • Focus on real-life activities and hobbies.
Visual Ideas
  • Checklist design
  • Wellness icons
  • Clean bright section styling
07

Try It Yourself Activity

Purpose
Encourage real-life application of the information learned.
Instructions
  1. Check your phone's screen time.
  2. Find your most-used app.
  3. Reflect on whether it improves or hurts your mood.
  4. Create a one-week goal for healthier use.
Goal Example
"I will avoid social media after 10 PM for one week."
04

Conclusion

04a Main Ideas
  • Social media is not entirely good or bad.
  • The effects depend on how it is used.
  • Awareness and balance are important.
  • Teens should learn to use social media intentionally instead of automatically.
  • Healthy habits can improve mental health and reduce stress.
04b Final Message

The goal is not to stop using technology, but to use it in a healthier and more controlled way.
